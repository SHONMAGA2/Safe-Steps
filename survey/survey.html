Survey Overview

A survey was conducted among learners to better understand road safety concerns around the school. A total of 181 responses were collected, providing a strong representation of learner experiences.

Grade Distribution

Responses were collected from all grade levels, with the majority coming from Grades 8, 9, and 10. Grade 12 learners were less represented, likely due to academic commitments.

Do Learners Feel Safe?

A large portion of learners reported feeling safe when crossing roads near the school. While this suggests a level of confidence among learners, it does not necessarily reflect the actual level of risk. Other findings, such as unsafe driver behaviour, indicate that safety concerns still exist.

Driver Behaviour

Survey responses indicate that drivers often do not follow road rules near the school, contributing significantly to the risk faced by learners.

Exposure to Dangerous Situations

A large number of learners reported witnessing dangerous situations near the school. However, fewer learners indicated that they had personally experienced such incidents.

This suggests that while direct involvement in accidents may be less common, the overall environment is still perceived as unsafe due to frequent observation of risky behaviour.

Key Findings

  • Many learners feel safe crossing roads near the school.
  • Dangerous situations are frequently witnessed by learners.
  • Fewer learners have directly experienced accidents, but the risk remains present.
  • Driver behaviour is a major contributing factor.
  • Current safety measures are not sufficient.
