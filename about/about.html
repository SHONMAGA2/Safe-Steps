The Road Safety Problem

Road Safety around the school has become a growing concern, with learners facing daily risks when crossing nearby roads. Unsafe driver behavior and limited infrastructure contribute significantly to this issue.

Problem Overview

Many learners around the school do not follow road safety rules and often cross roads unsafely, which increases the risk of accidents. In addition, some drivers fail to obey traffic regulations, putting learners in further danger. A recent incident where a learner was hit by a car, although not seriously injured, raised serious concerns about road safety around the school.

The lack of street patrols, traffic lights, visible signs, and general awareness contributes heavily to the ongoing road safety issues.

Root causes

A major cause of the problem is driver behaviour. Many drivers do not obey road rules near the school, which leads to speeding and increases the risk of injuring learners who are crossing the road.

Another issue is poor traffic management. Some drivers do not park in designated areas, causing congestion and making it difficult for traffic to move safely around the school.

Learner behaviour also plays a role. Groups of learners often gather near the school entrance and move close to the road, which puts them at risk of accidents.

Impact on learners

Learners are at constant risk of injury when crossing roads near the school.

Unsafe road behaviour disrupts their daily routines and makes travelling to and from school more stressful.

This situation can cause fear and anxiety among learners, especially when crossing busy roads.

Overall, this creates an unsafe environment that affects the well-being of the entire school community.

Why this needs urgent attention

Without proper enforcement of road safety measures, drivers will continue to ignore traffic rules, making the area around the school increasingly dangerous. If no action is taken, the risk of serious accidents will continue to grow, putting more learners in danger.
